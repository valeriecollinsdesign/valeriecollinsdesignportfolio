Wanderlust

Wanderlust is a part of who I am, and I wanted to create an app that helps fullfill that need. Enter Wanderlust. A responsive, location based app that eliminates the need for additional planning tools, so that the focus can be on enjoying the trip, and not worrying about where your itinerary went.

  • Adobe XD
  • InVision

Skills:

  • Critical Thinking
  • Active Listening
  • Lean UX Principals
  • Responsive Web Design

Presenting: The Case Study of Wanderlust

Follow along and I'll explain the process behind this creation. Be sure to stay until the end for the final screens!

This was one of the first projects I worked on as a student at Career Foundry. I was tasked with researching and designing a location based, responsive app.

The objective of the project was to create an application that solved the problem of having to use more than one tool to plan a trip. Whether it be short term or maybe longer

I did a competitive analysis of other similar apps to get an idea of what else was out there and to observe any weakness in the applications features and functions.

After completing the competitive analysis, I complied a list of their strenghts and weakness. And you know what? I found out that none of the apps I looked at had any of the features that I wanted to incorperate into my design.

With that in mind, I went to make my personas for the project. I interviewed several people, and made my personas with them in mind.

Most of my users were extremly busy people with kids who really enjoyed the outdoors and didnt have a lot of free time. So an application that not only helped them, but also made suggestions for them as well..... You can see how that would be helpful.

I found that not only would an intergration of suggestions would be helpful to my user, but keeping it all in one place made them all happy. As a designer, my goal is to design products with the user in mind.

With the user in mind, I sketched out my first set of low fidelity wire frames. And went into user testing to see how my flow was.

As the results came in from testing, I found out that the way the screens were designed were intuitive to the user. However, I did find out that some small details were missing. I needed to add a nav bar to some screens, and using a home button was more intuitive than using the logo for navigation to the home screen.

WIth the feedback in mind, I made my second set of frames and began adding in color to develop my style.

>

With a neutral color pallete set, I designed for different sized screens and breakpoints.

During the peer critique, I was offered more helpful advice than not. The main feedback was that my designs were outdated. Ouch. Which prompted me to redo my designs completly!

The final product!

I learned a lot during this project and I found out that as scary as peer critique is, its is needed to be able to grow and create better designs.
